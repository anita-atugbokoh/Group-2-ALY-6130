
Introduction
Canada Life's acquisition of Santé Circle Health creates opportunities for growth while also introducing strategic risks that require ongoing monitoring and management.
Risk 1 – Negative Risk
Problem Statement
Canada Life faces increasing competition from digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies. If Canada Life does not respond effectively, it may lose customers and experience slower growth.
Risk Statement
The risk of losing market share caused by increased competition from digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies, resulting in reduced customer acquisition, lower customer retention, and slower revenue growth.
Likelihood Assessment
The likelihood of this risk occurring is rated as 4 (High). Digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies continue to expand across Canada, increasing competition for traditional insurance organizations. The healthcare industry is becoming more technology-driven, making this a realistic and ongoing risk.
Impact Assessment
The impact of this risk is rated as 4 (High). If Canada Life loses market share, it could experience reduced revenue growth, lower profitability, and decreased customer retention. This may weaken its competitive position within the disability management and healthcare services market.
Risk Score
Likelihood Score: 4
Impact Score: 4
Risk Score: 16
Priority Level: High
Mitigation Strategy
Canada Life should invest in digital healthcare solutions, strengthen customer engagement programs, and expand partnerships with healthcare providers. The organization should also continue investing in innovation and technology to remain competitive within the evolving healthcare marketplace.
Risk Owner
Chief Strategy Officer
Conclusion
This risk requires continuous monitoring because competition within the healthcare industry is expected to increase. By investing in innovation and customer-focused services, Canada Life can reduce the likelihood of losing market share and maintain its position as a market leader.
