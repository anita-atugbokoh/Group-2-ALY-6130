Group Project for Risk Management Analytics

Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)
These KRIs are developed across the critical operational dimensions of people, processes, technology, facilities, and information security for Canada Life’s acquisition of Santé Circle Health.

People
Employee Turnover Rate Post-Acquisition: A turnover rate exceeding 15% within the first 12 months serves as a warning signal for cultural misalignment between the two organizations. To mitigate this risk, the integration plan includes retention bonuses, a structured cultural integration plan, and leadership alignment workshops.
Training Completion Rate for New Workflows: If fewer than 80% of employees complete training on new workflows within 90 days, this indicates a readiness gap that could impair operations. A mandatory onboarding program with compliance tracking is in place to ensure timely and consistent completion.

Process
Claims Processing Error Rate: An error rate above 5% signals a failure in process integration and warrants immediate intervention. Controls include a parallel processing period, standardized standard operating procedures (SOPs), and regular quality audits.
Regulatory Audit Findings: Any critical findings arising from OSFI or provincial regulatory audits represent a significant compliance risk. A dedicated compliance team and a pre-integration regulatory gap analysis have been established to prevent and address such findings proactively.

Technology
System Integration Downtime: More than four hours of unplanned system downtime per month following the merger is considered a threshold breach. An IT integration roadmap with redundant systems maintained throughout the transition period is designed to prevent extended outages.
Data Migration Accuracy Rate: A data accuracy rate below 99% during migration is a signal of migration failure and poses risks to data integrity. Third-party data validation and a phased migration approach with rollback capability are in place to safeguard accuracy.

Facilities
Service Delivery Disruption at Acquired Locations: Client complaints exceeding 10% above the pre-acquisition baseline indicate meaningful disruption to service delivery. A facility continuity plan and a proactive client communication strategy are deployed to maintain service quality and client confidence throughout the transition.
Information Security
PHI Access Violations: Any unauthorized access event involving Personal Health Information (PHI) triggers an immediate review. Controls include role-based access controls (RBAC), data encryption, and regular PIPEDA compliance audits to protect sensitive health data.
Cybersecurity Incident Response Time: A response time exceeding four hours to detect and contain a security breach is considered unacceptable given the sensitivity of health data involved. To address this, 24/7 Security Operations Centre (SOC) monitoring, a formal incident response plan, and annual penetration testing are in place.

Technical Risk
Legacy System Compatibility Failures: Any system incompatibility that blocks data exchange between Canada Life and Santé Circle Health systems represents a critical technical risk. Pre-acquisition IT due diligence and the development of an API integration layer are the primary controls designed to prevent and resolve such incompatibilities.
Preliminary Contributing Factors & Controls for Underlying Risks

Contributing Factors
1. The Cultural differences between Canada Life’s corporate insurance culture and Santé Circle Health’s healthcare services environment may slow integration and increase people risk.
2. Differing IT infrastructure and case management systems between the two organizations increase technical risk and the likelihood of data integrity issues during migration.
3. The sensitivity of personal health information (PHI) managed by Santé Circle Health significantly elevates information security and regulatory compliance risk.
4. Market entry into the active healthcare services sector exposes Canada Life to new competitive dynamics from digital health and telehealth providers.
5. Economic uncertainty and employer cost-cutting pressures may reduce demand for group disability management services post-acquisition.

Controls
1.	Establish a dedicated Integration Management Office (IMO) to oversee people, process, and technology integration with clearly defined milestones and KRI dashboards.
2.	Conduct a pre-integration IT due diligence review to identify system incompatibilities and develop a phased technology migration roadmap.
3.	Implement a comprehensive Data Governance Framework aligned with PIPEDA and Quebec’s Law 25, including role-based access controls, data encryption, and regular privacy audits.
4.	Deploy a Vendor and Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) program to assess information security risks inherited from Santé Circle Health’s existing supplier relationships.
5.	Establish a client communication and continuity plan to ensure no disruption to existing Santé Circle Health clients during the transition period.
6.	Conduct regular enterprise risk assessments using the ERM framework to monitor KRIs across all dimensions and escalate emerging risks to senior leadership.

Conclusion
The acquisition strategic opportunities for Canada Life but also introduces operational, technological and regulatory risks that require careful management through an effective ERM framework.
