## Introduction

Canada Life's acquisition of Santé Circle Health creates opportunities for growth while also introducing strategic risks that require ongoing monitoring and management. The healthcare and disability management industry is becoming increasingly competitive as digital healthcare providers, telehealth companies, and established insurance firms continue to expand their services. To remain competitive, Canada Life must identify and monitor key risks that could affect its market position and long-term growth.

## Risk 1 – Negative Risk

### Problem Statement

Canada Life faces increasing competition from digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies that continue to introduce innovative healthcare solutions. If Canada Life does not respond effectively to these competitors, it could lose customers and experience slower growth in the disability management market.

### Risk Statement

The risk of losing market share caused by increased competition from digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies, resulting in reduced customer acquisition, lower customer retention, and slower revenue growth.

### Likelihood Assessment

The likelihood of this risk occurring is rated as 4 (High). Digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies continue to expand across Canada, increasing competition for traditional insurance organizations. The healthcare industry is becoming more technology-driven, making this a realistic and ongoing risk.

### Impact Assessment

The impact of this risk is rated as 4 (High). If Canada Life loses market share, it could experience reduced revenue growth, lower profitability, and decreased customer retention. This may weaken its competitive position within the disability management and healthcare services market.

### Risk Score

- Likelihood Score: 4
- Impact Score: 4
- Risk Score: 16
- Priority Level: High

### Mitigation Strategy

Canada Life should invest in digital healthcare solutions, strengthen customer engagement programs, and expand partnerships with healthcare providers. The organization should continue investing in innovation and technology to remain competitive within the evolving healthcare marketplace.

### Risk Owner

Chief Strategy Officer

### Conclusion

This risk requires continuous monitoring because competition within the healthcare industry is expected to increase. By investing in innovation, strengthening customer relationships, and expanding digital healthcare capabilities, Canada Life can reduce the likelihood of losing market share and maintain its position as a market leader in disability management and healthcare services.


Risk 2 – Negative Risk
Problem Statement
Competitors may lower their prices or offer bundled healthcare and disability management solutions at reduced costs to attract customers. This competitive pricing pressure could make it difficult for Canada Life to maintain profit margins while remaining competitive in the marketplace.
Risk Statement
The risk of declining profit margins caused by competitors offering lower-cost healthcare and disability management services, resulting in reduced profitability and competitive disadvantage.
Likelihood Assessment
The likelihood of this risk occurring is rated as 4 (High). The healthcare and insurance industries are highly competitive, with organizations frequently using price reductions and bundled service offerings to attract and retain customers. As more competitors enter the market and seek market share, pricing pressure is likely to increase.
Impact Assessment
The impact of this risk is rated as 4 (High). Reduced profit margins could lower Canada Life's earnings and limit the company's ability to invest in technology, innovation, customer service improvements, and future growth initiatives. Sustained pricing pressure may also weaken the organization's long-term competitive position.
Risk Score
•	Likelihood Score: 4
•	Impact Score: 4
•	Risk Score: 16
•	Priority Level: High
Key Risk Indicator (KRI)
Competitor Pricing Difference
Level	Threshold
Green	Competitor pricing within 5% of Canada Life pricing

Mitigation Strategy
Canada Life should focus on differentiating its services through superior customer support, advanced disability management programs, digital healthcare solutions, and value-added services. The company should continuously monitor competitor pricing, improve operational efficiency, and communicate the quality and benefits of its offerings to reduce reliance on price competition.
Risk Owner
Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
Conclusion
This risk requires ongoing monitoring because aggressive competitor pricing can significantly affect profitability and market competitiveness. By strengthening its value proposition, improving operational efficiency, and investing in innovative healthcare solutions, Canada Life can reduce the impact of pricing pressure while maintaining sustainable profit margins and long-term growth.
