# Module 2 Risk Identification

## Introduction

Canada Life's acquisition of Santé Circle Health creates opportunities for growth while also introducing strategic risks that require ongoing monitoring and management. The healthcare and disability management industry is becoming increasingly competitive as digital healthcare providers, telehealth companies, and established insurance firms continue to expand their services. To remain competitive, Canada Life must identify and monitor key risks that could affect its market position and long-term growth.

## Risk 1 – Negative Risk

### Problem Statement

Canada Life faces increasing competition from digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies that continue to introduce innovative healthcare solutions. If Canada Life does not respond effectively to these competitors, it could lose customers and experience slower growth in the disability management market.

### Risk Statement

The risk of losing market share caused by increased competition from digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies, resulting in reduced customer acquisition, lower customer retention, and slower revenue growth.

### Likelihood Assessment

The likelihood of this risk occurring is rated as 4 (High). Digital healthcare providers and telehealth companies continue to expand across Canada, increasing competition for traditional insurance organizations. The healthcare industry is becoming more technology-driven, making this a realistic and ongoing risk.

### Impact Assessment

The impact of this risk is rated as 4 (High). If Canada Life loses market share, it could experience reduced revenue growth, lower profitability, and decreased customer retention. This may weaken its competitive position within the disability management and healthcare services market.

### Risk Score

- Likelihood Score: 4
- Impact Score: 4
- Risk Score: 16
- Priority Level: High

### Mitigation Strategy

Canada Life should invest in digital healthcare solutions, strengthen customer engagement programs, and expand partnerships with healthcare providers. The organization should continue investing in innovation and technology to remain competitive within the evolving healthcare marketplace.

### Risk Owner

Chief Strategy Officer

### Conclusion

This risk requires continuous monitoring because competition within the healthcare industry is expected to increase. By investing in innovation, strengthening customer relationships, and expanding digital healthcare capabilities, Canada Life can reduce the likelihood of losing market share and maintain its position as a market leader in disability management and healthcare services.
