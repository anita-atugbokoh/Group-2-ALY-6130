# Module 3 Risk Assessment – Risk Heat Map Update

## Updated Heat Map Analysis

Following the completion of the Risk Calculation Sheet and Risk Register, the Risk Heat Map has been refined using the finalized likelihood and impact scores for the Canada Life acquisition of Santé Circle Health.

### Risk Classification Summary

The risk assessment identified risks across financial, operational, competitive, reputational, technical, and legal/regulatory categories. Based on the established scoring framework:

| Risk Level | Score Range | Color |
|------------|------------|--------|
| Low | 1–8 | Green |
| Medium | 9–14 | Yellow |
| High | 15–25 | Red |

### Preliminary Heat Map Findings

Based on the current risk assessment, several risks are expected to fall within the High-Risk category and will require immediate management attention.

Key high-priority risks include:

- Cross-selling revenue synergies failing to meet projections.
- Goodwill write-down resulting from unrealized acquisition benefits.
- Loss of key Santé Circle Health employees during integration.
- Competitive pressure from established insurers and digital healthcare providers.
- Technology integration challenges between Canada Life and Santé Circle Health systems.
- Privacy, data governance, and regulatory compliance risks.

These risks are expected to occupy the upper-right section of the heat map where both likelihood and impact are high.

### Heat Map Interpretation

The preliminary analysis indicates that Canada Life’s greatest exposure is concentrated in competitive, operational, technical, and legal/regulatory risk categories. The acquisition introduces opportunities for growth; however, successful integration will depend on effective change management, employee retention, technology modernization, regulatory compliance, and customer retention strategies.

The heat map will allow management to quickly identify risk priorities and allocate resources toward the most significant threats facing the organization.

### Alignment with Group Deliverables

The Risk Heat Map will be developed using:

- Risk scores from the Risk Calculation Sheet.
- Risk descriptions and mitigation strategies from the Risk Register.
- High, Medium, and Low thresholds established by the project team.

This ensures consistency across all Module 3 deliverables and supports a comprehensive risk assessment process.

### Next Steps

The final heat map will be created in both Excel and Python using the validated likelihood and impact scores from the Risk Calculation Sheet. Risks will be plotted according to their scores and categorized using the approved threshold ranges. The completed heat map will be included in the final Module 3 submission.
