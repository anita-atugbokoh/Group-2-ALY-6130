# Abigail Dabanka-Frimpong – EDA and Monte Carlo Simulation Contribution

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Performed exploratory data analysis to understand the characteristics and behavior of the datasets used in the Canada Life acquisition risk assessment.

The analysis included:

- Data cleaning and preparation.
- Descriptive statistics and summary measures.
- Visualization of risk distributions and patterns.
- Examination of relationships among variables.
- Identification of trends and outliers.

The EDA findings supported the selection of three critical risks:

- R-003 Cross-Selling Revenue Shortfall
- R-010 PHI Data Breach
- R-013 Legacy System Incompatibility

## Monte Carlo Simulation

Performed Monte Carlo simulation to quantify uncertainty and estimate financial exposure associated with the selected risks.

Responsibilities included:

- Defining simulation assumptions.
- Running 10,000 iterations.
- Calculating P10, P50 and P90 values.
- Evaluating expected losses.
- Producing probability distributions and statistical outputs.

## Key Findings

### R-003 Cross-Selling Revenue Shortfall

Monte Carlo simulation showed a median outcome of approximately CAD 3.3 million, far below the expected CAD 18 million target.

### R-010 PHI Data Breach

Estimated median loss was approximately CAD 10.6 million.

### R-013 Legacy System Incompatibility

Estimated median loss was approximately CAD 3.7 million.

## Deliverables

- EDA notebook.
- Monte Carlo simulation notebook.
- Statistical visualizations.
- Distribution analysis.
- Quantitative insights supporting risk response and decision-making.

These analyses transformed qualitative risk assessment into evidence-based decision support and provided a foundation for risk response strategies and Key Risk Indicators (KRIs).



# Second Edit – Expanded EDA Findings and Monte Carlo Analysis

## Exploratory Data Analysis Findings

A more detailed review of the datasets was conducted to assess data quality and suitability for quantitative risk analysis.

### Data Quality Assessment

The datasets were examined for:

- Missing values in key variables.
- Duplicate records.
- Data type consistency.
- Outliers and unusual observations.
- Consistency of risk identifiers and categories.

The review indicated that the datasets were generally suitable for analysis after minor cleaning and validation procedures.

### Descriptive Analysis

Exploratory analysis revealed that the majority of risks were concentrated within the medium-to-high impact categories. Several risks showed elevated likelihood scores, indicating the need for additional quantitative assessment.

The distribution of risk scores suggested that a small number of risks contributed disproportionately to overall organizational exposure.

## Monte Carlo Simulation Methodology

Monte Carlo simulation was selected to quantify uncertainty and estimate potential outcomes for high-priority risks.

### Simulation Process

The simulation process included:

1. Identifying critical risk variables.
2. Defining probability distributions.
3. Running 10,000 simulation iterations.
4. Calculating expected outcomes.
5. Evaluating risk variability and confidence ranges.

### Key Results

#### R-003 Cross-Selling Revenue Shortfall

Simulation results showed a median outcome of approximately CAD 3.3 million compared with the projected CAD 18 million target.

This finding suggests a significant gap between expected and achievable revenue synergies.

#### R-010 PHI Data Breach

The analysis estimated a median financial impact of approximately CAD 10.6 million.

The results highlight the importance of cybersecurity controls and regulatory compliance.

#### R-013 Legacy System Incompatibility

The simulation estimated a median loss of approximately CAD 3.7 million.

The findings emphasize the need for phased implementation and careful technology integration planning.

## Contribution to Risk Management

The EDA and Monte Carlo analyses provide quantitative evidence that supports:

- Risk prioritization.
- Strategic decision-making.
- Resource allocation.
- Development of mitigation strategies.
- Continuous monitoring through Key Risk Indicators (KRIs).

These analyses strengthen the overall enterprise risk management framework by transforming uncertainty into measurable and actionable insights.
